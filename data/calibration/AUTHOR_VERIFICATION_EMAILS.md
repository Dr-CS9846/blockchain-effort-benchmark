# Author-Verification Email Templates

approval.** Every send + response is logged in the intake register (verification block).*

## T1 — Initial verification email

**Subject:** Quick verification of {{project_name}}'s development effort — academic effort-estimation study

Dear {{author_name}},

I'm a researcher building an open, fully auditable dataset to calibrate software cost-estimation models
(COCOMO II) to real blockchain/open-source development. {{project_name}} is one of the projects in the set,
selected because its funded work and source repository are clearly matched and publicly verifiable.

To make sure the record is accurate, could you confirm or correct a few specifics? It should take only a
minute or two:

1. **Total effort.** Our record lists **{{reported_effort_verbatim}}** ({{reported_PM}} person-months) for the
   funded work. Is that right? If you logged actual hours, the actual figure is what we most want.
2. **The exact code delivered.** We attribute the effort to **{{repo_url}}** (commit/range {{pinned_sha}}).
   Is that the single repository — and roughly the commit range — that this funding produced?
3. **Team.** How many people worked on it, and roughly their seniority?
4. **Reuse.** Was a meaningful share forked from a template, generated by tooling, or imported from another
   project (vs. written from scratch)? A rough fraction is fine.
5. **Actual vs. budgeted.** Is {{reported_effort_verbatim}} the *actual* effort spent, or the *budgeted/proposed*
   figure from the application?

Your data will appear in an openly published, version-controlled dataset; I'm happy to share the entry and the
final results with you, and to attribute or anonymize your confirmation as you prefer.

Thank you very much for your time.

{{researcher_name}}
{{affiliation}} · {{contact_back}}

## T2 — Follow-up (only for doubtful data with no response; +7 days, then +21 days)

**Subject:** Re: Quick verification of {{project_name}}'s development effort

Dear {{author_name}},

Gently following up on the note below — I'd value your confirmation because {{project_name}} sits at a point in
the dataset where the public record is a little ambiguous on **{{specific_doubt}}**, and your answer would
materially improve the accuracy of the study. Even a one-line reply ("looks right" / "actually it was X")
would help enormously.

If I don't hear back, I'll proceed using the public on-chain/proposal record and flag the entry accordingly.
No action needed on your part in that case.

With thanks,
{{researcher_name}}

## Sending rules (from the Charter §6)
- **Email everyone we admit.** T1 to all; T2 only to *doubtful-data non-responders*.
- Doubt-free non-responders: T1 once, then proceed flagged **"public-record only."**
- Max two follow-ups (+7d, +21d). Log every send date + outcome.
- `author-corrected` replies overwrite the figure; the original is preserved in `change_log.md`.
- **Draft + queue only — user approves each batch before it is sent.**
